Classic Napa Cabbage Kimchi

  • Prep 30 min
  • Ferment 4 days
  • Total ~72 hrs
  • Yield 8 cups
  • Sylvia Fountaine, Feasting at Home
Servings 16

Ingredients

  • 2 lb napa cabbage (1 large head)
  • ¼ cup sea salt (60 g)
  • 2 cups daikon radish, matchstick-cut (or carrots)
  • 1 bunch scallions, trimmed, cut 1"
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, sliced 2-3 disks
  • 6 cloves garlic, whole
  • 1 — shallot, quartered (optional)
  • 4 tbsp Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (or miso/soy for vegan)
  • 2 tsp sugar (or honey, brown rice syrup)
  • 1 tbsp glutinous rice powder (optional)

Steps

  1. 1

    Salt the cabbage ⏱ 6-8 hrs

    Reserve 1-2 outer leaves, refrigerate. Core and cut remaining cabbage into 1-inch pieces. Toss with salt in a large bowl. Add cool water to cover. Submerge with a plate. Stand at room temp 6-8 hours, stir midway if possible.

  2. 2

    Drain & rinse

    Drain cabbage, save the brine. Rinse lightly, drain, squeeze/blot excess water. Return to bowl with daikon and scallions.

  3. 3

    Make the paste

    Food processor: ginger, garlic, shallot, gochugaru, fish sauce, sugar, optional rice powder. Pulse until thick paste.

  4. 4

    Massage

    Wearing gloves or using tongs, mix paste into vegetables thoroughly until well coated.

  5. 5

    Pack jars

    Pack into a large 2-quart jar or crock, leaving 1-2 inches headroom. Add reserved brine to just cover. Place whole cabbage leaf on top to keep kimchi submerged. Use fermentation weight or water-filled zip-lock.

  6. 6

    Ferment ⏱ 3-4 days

    Cover loosely. Place jar in baking dish for overflow. Store dark and cool (55-65 °F ideal) for 3 days.

  7. 7

    Check fermentation ⏱ Day 3 evening

    Tap jar — look for bubbles. Bubbles = ready to fridge. No bubbles = wait 1-2 more days. For tangier: ferment 3 more days.

  8. 8

    Refrigerate

    Move to fridge. Full flavor at ~2 weeks. Keeps for months if submerged.

Notes

2 tbsp gochugaru = mild • 4 tbsp = medium • 6 tbsp = spicy. Adjust to taste.
Cook 1 tbsp glutinous rice powder with ½ cup water over medium heat until thickened. Cool completely, then add to the paste. This helps the kimchi develop a better texture and feeds the fermentation.
If you need more brine to keep kimchi submerged: 1 cup water + 1¼ tsp fine sea salt. Mix until dissolved.
If you see mold on the surface, remove the top leaf, wipe the rim clean, and replace with a fresh leaf. Always keep kimchi fully submerged under brine.
Check the temperature — ideal range is 50-70 °F (60-65 °F is the sweet spot). Too cold slows fermentation. Give it another day or two.
Use a slotted spoon to serve. Top with sesame oil, toasted sesame seeds, and fresh scallions.

Lemon Kimchi with Korean Red Chili Sauce & Charred Scallions

  • Yield 30 servings
  • Chef Robert Dahni
Servings 30

Lemon Kimchi Ingredients

  • 1 lb lemons, sliced ⅛" thin, no seeds
  • 1 lb napa cabbage, cut 1" squares
  • 3 oz kosher or sea salt
  • 2 tbsp Korean coarse red pepper powder
  • 2 tbsp garlic, minced
  • ½ tbsp ginger, minced
  • 4 — scallions, minced & charred
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar

Korean Red Chili Sauce

  • ½ cup Korean red chili paste (gochujang)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp soju or sake
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp garlic, fresh chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger, fresh chopped
  • 1 tsp orange zest

Steps

  1. 1

    Salt lemons & cabbage ⏱ 4 hrs

    Toss lemons and cabbage with salt. Transfer to a noncorrosive container. Marinate at room temp for 4 hours.

  2. 2

    Rinse & drain

    Quick rinse with cool running water, drain well.

  3. 3

    Mix kimchi

    Toss drained lemons and cabbage with pepper powder, garlic, ginger, charred scallions, fish sauce, and sugar. Place in a noncorrosive container, weigh down.

  4. 4

    Ferment at room temp ⏱ 3-4 days

    Store at room temperature until desired cure, usually 3-4 days.

  5. 5

    Refrigerate

    Remove weight, store covered. Ferment further in fridge 1-3 weeks.

  6. 6

    Make the red chili sauce

    Blend all sauce ingredients until smooth. Transfer to a squeeze bottle.

  7. 7

    Assemble & serve

    Per serving: 2 tbsp lemon kimchi + 8 pieces charred scallion (½" pieces, mostly white parts) + ¼ oz Korean red chili sauce.

Notes

Char scallions in a cast iron pan, under a broiler, or on a grill until blistered and softened. The white parts are preferred for charring — they get sweet and smoky.
Arrange lemon kimchi on a plate, scatter charred scallion pieces, and drizzle red chili sauce from the squeeze bottle for a clean, restaurant-style presentation.
